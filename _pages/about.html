My story

I grew up drawn to the intersection of ideas and action — the place where a sharp message meets the right market at exactly the right moment. That instinct led me into marketing, and two decades of working in cybersecurity have only sharpened it.

I have spent my career at the early and growth stages of companies where the playbook does not yet exist. At Secureworks, I helped build the marketing function as the company scaled from $8M to over $100M ARR through its acquisition by Dell. At PhishLabs, I did it again — taking the company from under $2M to $26M ARR and positioning it as a category leader in Digital Risk Protection ahead of its acquisition by Fortra. Most recently at Fortra, I led marketing for a $200M+ ARR portfolio of defensive security solutions.

Two exits. Three companies. One consistent throughline: building the kind of GTM engine that makes growth inevitable and acquisition logical.

I do not inherit strategies. I build them — and I build the teams that execute them.
