My story

I grew up in South Carolina, about 35 miles from Myrtle Beach — out in the country, where after-school activities included caring for a herd of goats and hunting dogs, cleaning up after hurricanes, and being trusted with a chainsaw at an age that would make a modern parent fairly nervous. Small public high school, under 100 kids per class. Marching band, baseball, honors student. And the summer before my senior year, I started dating the woman who would eventually become my wife.

At Coastal Carolina University I commuted from home my freshman year and waited tables to make ends meet. Eventually landed an internship at LURHQ — one of the first managed security services providers, conveniently headquartered in Myrtle Beach. I graduated cum laude with a double major in management and marketing, married my college girlfriend in a small ceremony at the family church, and honeymooned in Gatlinburg, Tennessee — which I still refer to as the Myrtle Beach of the Smokies. Went from intern to full-time Marketing Coordinator without breaking stride.

When LURHQ merged with SecureWorks, my wife and I moved to Atlanta. SecureWorks needed product marketing, and inside of me is a giant geek who likes to argue and explain things — so I became their first product marketer and eventually built out the team. We also built out our family: three kids in the span of a few years, no extended family nearby, mostly figuring it out as we went. The marketing team at SecureWorks was incredibly supportive through all of it. When Dell acquired SecureWorks, a lot changed. Eventually, the call came to lead marketing for PhishLabs in Charleston — a chance to build from scratch, closer to family. It was a hard choice. We made it.

We spent nearly a decade in Charleston, building PhishLabs from a founder-led operation into a scalable business that was eventually acquired by HelpSystems. Meanwhile, our family was growing fast in every sense — three kids in constant motion, a Corgi rescue dog, and weekend escapes up I-26 to the mountains of western North Carolina that slowly got longer and more frequent. We fell in love with that part of the world. When our oldest was finishing elementary school, we had a narrow window: move before the kids got too old to uproot or stay put forever. We moved.

The WNC years have been full — in the best possible way. We've added horses to the family (yes, horses; I had hoped that years of barn chores would cool the enthusiasm, and I was wrong). The kids play viola, trumpet, and oboe. Our oldest wraps up her senior year this spring and heads to college in the fall, which is bringing up all kinds of feelings in our household. In February 2026, Fortra and I parted ways. I'm grateful for the experience and the many great people there. But it was time. At this point in my career, I know where I excel, what I value most in the companies I join, and the kind of teams I do my best work alongside. I'm ready for what comes next.
