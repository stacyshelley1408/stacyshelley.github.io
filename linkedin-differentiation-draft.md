# LinkedIn Post Draft: Differentiation — The Macro and the Micro

---

"We need to work on our differentiation" sounds specific. It's not.

It can mean two very different things. Confusing them leads to solving the wrong problem.

I think of it as macro and micro. Less economics, more gaming. If you've played Civ or Starcraft, you know: macro is what you do to build structural advantage. Micro is what you do to win the fight in front of you. You need both.

In cybersecurity GTM, your macro-differentiation is what gets you in the room. It's your brand-level positioning. What makes you memorable. What gives a prospect a reason to look at you over whoever they're already using.

Your micro-differentiation is what wins the eval. The specific features that are unique to you or that outperform the competition where it matters most.

Both matter. But they matter at different points in the process.

Early in the buyer's journey, it's the macro. Does your positioning stand out enough to warrant attention?

Later, when prospects are comparing you side-by-side with alternatives, micro is what closes the deal.

Here's what took me a while to fully appreciate: macro doesn't fade as the deal progresses. It starts the conversation and creates the ideal conditions for your micro to land. Strong macro primes buyers to see your specific capabilities as more meaningful than the same feature from a competitor.

They're not separate. They're mutually reinforcing.

So when someone says "we need better differentiation," the first question is: where in the process?

Front-end problem? Look at the macro.
Back-end problem? Look at the micro.

Different diagnosis. Different treatment.
